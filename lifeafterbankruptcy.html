Life after Bankruptcy

Are you a person worried that filing bankruptcy will ruin your credit? That is a good question and worth taking a look and getting the true answer.

As a person may know, after filing for bankruptcy a person is free and clear, and a person will have no outstanding debt but a person cannot file for bankruptcy again for another eight years. The truth is a person looks much better to the credit card companies after a person has finished filing bankruptcy.

Many Studies say that 90% of individuals who have filed bankruptcy receive solicitation from a lender actually indicating that they knew they had filed bankruptcy. Credit is always available somehow. So don't worry. Remember, credit is a persons ability to borrow money, and the credit score may actually improve after filing bankruptcy.

Are you a person considering Buying a home after bankruptcy.

There are many considerations in determining whether or not to lend to a person after filing bankruptcy. First a person must understand what banks consider when determining whether or not to loan a person money. First and foremost the debt to income ratio must be discussed. Probably the most important factor that they consider is a persons debt to income ratio, in other words, out much outstanding debt a person has relative to a persons income coming in.

Does bankruptcy give a negative perception?

Filing bankruptcy is a very serious subject. Whether a person pays a persons bills does not determine whether or not a person is a good person. We think that other factors like how a person treats a person in the family or what values are important to a person are more important factors.

Most of the time people file bankruptcy because of circumstances beyond their control. In fact, statistics show that over 70% of all bankruptcies are due to loss of job, divorce, or medical problems that are uninsured. So does that really make a person a bad person? Because things happen to a person that are beyond a persons control?
